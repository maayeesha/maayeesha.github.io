I

What I am

On language, bias & the soul of machines
LLM Evaluation AI Fairness NLP Responsible AI Hate Speech Low-Resource Languages Computational Social Science

My research explores how AI systems interact with society — specifically how large language models encode preferences, biases, and cultural assumptions, and what that means for equitable technology.

At Université Paris Dauphine – PSL, I work as a Research Assistant with the ACSS-PSL institute and the Compar:IA team (French Ministry of Culture). My current work analyzes how readability and stylistic features influence user preferences for LLM responses, using a Bradley–Terry model to predict pairwise outcomes.

Previously, at Chattogram Veterinary & Animal Sciences University, I contributed to genomics research on Bangladeshi isolates of Anaplasma marginale, building bioinformatics pipelines and presenting at the 36th Molecular Parasitology Meeting at the Marine Biological Laboratory in Massachusetts.

My undergraduate thesis at CUET focused on multiclass Bengali audio hate speech classification — building ML/DL pipelines for a low-resource language and applying explainable AI (SHAP) to interpret model decisions.
