01 — Research

Research Statement

Research Interests
LLM Evaluation AI Fairness & Bias NLP Responsible AI Hate Speech Detection Low-Resource Languages AI & Society Computational Social Science

My research explores how AI systems interact with society — specifically how large language models encode preferences, biases, and cultural assumptions, and what that means for equitable technology. I’m driven by the belief that AI should be both intelligent and socially meaningful.

At Université Paris Dauphine – PSL, I work as a Research Assistant in collaboration with the ACSS-PSL institute and the Compar:IA team, a platform developed under the French Ministry of Culture. My current work analyzes how readability and stylistic features influence user preferences for LLM responses, using a Bradley–Terry model to predict pairwise outcomes and refine evaluation methodologies.

Previously, at Chattogram Veterinary and Animal Sciences University, I contributed to genomics research on Bangladeshi isolates of Anaplasma marginale, developing bioinformatics pipelines with Bowtie, BCFTools, Roary, and R. This interdisciplinary experience deepened my appreciation for data-driven approaches across domains.

My undergraduate thesis at CUET focused on multiclass Bengali audio hate speech classification, building ML/DL pipelines for a low-resource language and applying explainable AI (SHAP) to interpret model decisions — work that directly connects to my interest in fair and transparent AI.

For my PhD, I want to investigate how LLMs can be evaluated and improved for fairness, cultural sensitivity, and social impact. I’m particularly excited about building evaluation frameworks for multilingual and low-resource settings, understanding preference formation in human-AI interaction, and developing socially responsible AI systems.
